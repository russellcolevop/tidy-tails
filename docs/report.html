The headline story

This business has grown from a $35K annualized pace in 2023 to $48,182 in 2025 — a real, compounding upward trend. Monthly revenue has increased 39% over two years.

The growth is coming from two sources: deeper loyalty from existing clients, and a steady stream of new accounts converting to regulars. Ren's Pets, Wallis/Borrelli, and Leigh Majury are all examples of accounts that barely existed in 2023 and are now anchors.

The ceiling is becoming a capacity question, not a demand question. At $4,015/month average in 2025, the business is approaching the point where a second groomer stops being optional.

New client retention problem

Roughly 30–40 new client names appear each year and never return. At an average ticket of $80, winning back just 10 of them for 2 more visits is worth $1,600 in annual revenue. First visits are a marketing cost — the payoff is repeat business. A simple 6-week follow-up text would recover a significant portion of these.

Why the rate dipped in 2024

The 2024 tip rate of 6.5% looks like a problem but isn't. It reflects new commercial clients (Ren's Pets, batch accounts) joining who don't tip on the same terms as individual clients. The loyal client base actually tipped the same or more in 2024. The rate recovered to 7.4% in 2025 as those clients matured. The trend is healthy.

Zero-tip strategy

Kathleen Shaughnessy (19 visits, rarely tips), Lesley Santos (13 visits, rarely tips), and Joanna Pesard (variable) are your high-volume, low-tip clients. Don't confront it — price it. A selective rate increase for these clients normalizes revenue. Add a gratuity prompt to invoices and capture the accidental under-tippers first.

What gets you to $60,000+

Adding a second groomer for 1–2 days per week is the only path to $60K+. At current pricing, Samantha and the team are approaching maximum throughput. The demand is there — Ren's Pets alone is scaling toward $3,000+/year, Wallis/Borrelli doubled their visits in one year, and new clients are arriving consistently. The constraint is time, not customers. A second dedicated groomer at even 20 appointments per week adds $1,600–$2,000 in weekly revenue capacity. That is the $60K unlock.
