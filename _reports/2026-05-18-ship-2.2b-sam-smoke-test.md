# Tidy Tails v2 — first-day smoke test

A hands-on, ~10-minute check that the new app actually works. Run it the first
time you sign in to v2 after the switch-over. It is written to be done by
**Samantha**; **Russell** runs the same checks at cutover before handing the
app over.

This is the *functional* walkthrough — "does each feature work when I use it."
The operator's "is the environment ready / red-flag" checklist is
`2026-05-18-ship-2.2b-final-precutover-check.md` §4; this doc does not repeat it.

## How to use this

- Go through the checks **in order**. Each one says: **do this →** what you
  **should see →** what to do **if it's wrong**.
- If a check is wrong, **don't work around it.** Note exactly what you did and
  what you saw, and tell Russell (see *What to report* at the end). A small
  thing flagged early is easy to fix.
- The app address is **https://tidy-tails-v2.vercel.app**.

## A. Sign in

**Do this:** Open the app address. Sign in with your email
(`sammclennan143@gmail.com`) and your password.

**You should see:** The home screen — a search box and your list/dashboard.

**If it's wrong:** If sign-in fails or the screen is blank, stop and tell
Russell right away. Don't keep trying — this is the one check that blocks
everything else.

## B. Find a client (look-up)

**Do this:** In the search box, type part of a client's **last name**. Then
clear it and try part of a **phone number**. Then try a **pet's name**.

**You should see:** Matching clients appear as you type. Searching by last
name, phone, or pet name all find people. Two clients with the same surname,
or two pets with the same name, are still told apart on the result cards.

**If it's wrong:** A search that finds nothing for a client you know exists, or
results that never appear — note the exact text you typed and report it.

## C. Open a client (their detail page)

**Do this:** Tap one of the search results to open a client.

**You should see:** Their name, phone (tappable to call), any alternate contact
and address on file, any notes, their pets, and an appointment history below.

**If it's wrong:** Missing pets, a blank history for a client you know has
visited, or wrong contact details — report which client and what's off.

## D. Open a pet

**Do this:** From a client's page, tap one of their pets.

**You should see:** The pet's details — breed, and any allergy alert and
grooming notes. An allergy shows as a clear warning. The "last groom" date, if
shown, is a **past** date — never a future one.

**If it's wrong:** An allergy that you know exists but isn't shown is the most
important thing to flag. Also report a "last groom" date that is in the future.

## E. Reports

**Do this:** Open the **Reports** screen.

**You should see:** This month's totals — number of visits and revenue — and
the numbers look about right for your business.

**If it's wrong:** A revenue total that looks far too high or low, or a visit
count that's clearly off — report the screen and the number you expected.

## F. The four action buttons — on day one they are OFF (this is normal)

**Do this:** On a client's page, try each of the four actions in turn — **Add
Appointment**, **Log Groom**, **Reminder**, **Add Household**. Fill one in and
submit it.

**You should see:** The form works fully, then a message like *"…isn't switched
on yet — it turns on soon after launch. Nothing was saved."* — **this is
expected.** The four buttons are switched on **one at a time over the first
week or so**, each after a check. Nothing you do here saves yet.

**If it's wrong:** If a button *errors* or *crashes* (rather than showing the
calm "not switched on yet" message), report it. If one *does* save something on
day one when it should still be off, tell Russell immediately.

## G. When a button gets switched on

Russell will tell you as each action button goes live (Add Appointment first,
then Log Groom, then Reminder, then Add Household). The first time you use a
**newly switched-on** button:

**Do this:** Use it once for real and submit.

**You should see:** A **"Saved"** confirmation (not the "not switched on yet"
message). Then open that client again and confirm the new appointment / groom /
household actually shows up.

**If it's wrong:** It says "Saved" but the new entry doesn't appear — or it
shows an error — report it the same day. (Russell's side: the per-flip
mini-verifier in `2026-05-18-ship-2.2b-flips-1-2-operator-cards.md`.)

**Reminders are different — and stay different forever:** a reminder text is
**only ever sent when you tap a confirm button for that one message.** There is
no automatic, scheduled, or "send all" sending — ever. If a text goes out that
you did **not** confirm, that is a serious problem — tell Russell at once.

## What to report if something's wrong

For anything that fails, send Russell:

| Tell him | Example |
|---|---|
| Which check (A–G) | "Check C — opening a client" |
| What you did | "Tapped Marisol Park from the search results" |
| What you saw | "Her two dogs weren't listed" |
| What you expected | "She has Bella and one other" |
| When | "About 9:15 this morning" |

A screenshot helps. **Don't** keep re-trying a broken thing or invent a
workaround — one clear report is worth more than ten retries.

| Symptom | What to do |
|---|---|
| Can't sign in / blank screen | Stop. Tell Russell now — this blocks everything. |
| A button **errors or crashes** | Report it; avoid that button until Russell says it's fixed. |
| A button **saved** on day one (should be off) | Tell Russell immediately. |
| An SMS went out you didn't confirm | Tell Russell **immediately** — this is a hard rule. |
| Data looks wrong (missing pet, wrong total) | Report which client/screen and what you expected. |
| Anything just feels off | Report it anyway. Early and small beats late and big. |

The old site (v1) is retired at cutover — v2 is the tool now. If v2 is unusable
for core work and there's no quick fix, Russell can roll back; that decision is
his, not a workaround for you to find.
