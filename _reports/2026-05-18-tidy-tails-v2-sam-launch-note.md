# Tidy Tails — your new app is ready

Hi Samantha — this is the short version of what changes, what to do first, and
what to expect over the first week. Nothing here needs any technical knowledge.

## What's happening

You've been using the old Tidy Tails site. We've built a new one — faster,
mobile-first, made to match the way you actually work through a grooming day.
On cutover day the old site is switched off and the new app becomes your tool.

The new app is **https://tidy-tails-v2.vercel.app** — open it on your phone and
"Add to Home Screen" so it sits next to your other apps.

## How to log in

1. Open **https://tidy-tails-v2.vercel.app**.
2. Sign in with your email **sammclennan143@gmail.com** and your password.
3. That's it — you'll land on your home screen.

If the login doesn't work, don't worry and don't keep retrying — just tell
Russell. Your account is already set up; it's a quick fix on our side.

## What you can do on day one

From day one the app is your **lookup cockpit** — everything about your clients
and pets, instantly:

- **Search** any client or pet by name and open their file.
- See each pet's **safety card** — allergies, temperament, vaccination status.
- See a client's **appointment history** and when they last came in.
- See your **lapsed clients** — who's overdue for a visit.
- See your **revenue** — what you've earned, by month.

All of this is live, real data — your actual business, on your phone.

## What is not switched on yet — and why

The app can also **add appointments, log grooms, send reminders, and add new
households**. On day one those four buttons are deliberately **switched off** —
you'll see a short "not switched on yet" message if you try them.

This is on purpose, not a bug. We switch them on **one at a time over the first
week or so**, checking each one works perfectly before moving to the next.
Russell will tell you as each goes live. Until a button is on, keep doing that
one task the way you do today (e.g. note a new booking as usual) — you won't
lose anything.

The order they switch on: **Add Appointment → Log Groom → Send Reminder → Add
Household.**

## What to test first

When you first log in, spend five minutes doing the things you do every day:

1. Search for **three clients you know well** — do their details look right?
2. Open a pet you know — is the **allergy / vaccination** info correct?
3. Open **Reports** — does the revenue total look about right for you?
4. Check **lapsed clients** — does that list make sense?

You're just sanity-checking that your real data looks correct. It should.

## How reminders work

When the reminder feature switches on, the app **prepares** a text for you —
it writes the draft and shows it to you. **Nothing is sent until you read it
and tap to confirm**, one message at a time. The app will **never** send a text
on its own, never in a batch, never on a schedule. You are always the one who
hits send.

## Money and payments

The app **tracks** money — every appointment's fee and tip is recorded, and you
can see your totals and monthly revenue. It does **not collect** payments.
You're still paid the way you are today — cash or e-transfer at the
appointment. Card payments and saved cards are a possible later addition, not
part of this launch.

## Spreadsheets

Exporting your data to a spreadsheet is a feature we'll add a little later — it
is not part of this launch and nothing depends on it. You don't need to import
any spreadsheet; your data is already in the app.

## If something looks wrong

If anything looks off — a client's details wrong, a number that doesn't make
sense, a screen that won't load — **don't try to fix it and don't keep tapping**.
Just tell Russell what you saw and roughly when. If it's a real problem we can
put the old site back the same day; that safety net is in place for the whole
first week.

Welcome to v2 — this is built for your day.
